# Destino SF — Website Care Plan

**Period:** April – September 2026
**Prepared:** April 6, 2026

---

## Why Maintenance Matters

A website is like a restaurant kitchen — even when everything is running smoothly, it needs regular cleaning, equipment checks, and occasional upgrades to stay safe and efficient. Without ongoing care, small issues quietly build up until they become expensive problems.

This plan keeps your website secure, fast, and reliable while preparing it for growth.

---

## What Happens Every Month

These are routine activities that keep everything running smoothly:

- **Security checkup** — Scan for and fix any new vulnerabilities
- **Software updates** — Apply bug fixes and small improvements
- **Backup verification** — Make sure your data is being safely backed up
- **Performance check** — Verify pages are loading quickly for customers
- **Error review** — Investigate and resolve any recurring issues

---

## Month-by-Month Plan

### April — Go Live & Set Up Monitoring

Deploy all the improvements we just completed (security, speed, testing) to the live website. Set up monitoring so we get alerted immediately if anything goes wrong.

**What you'll notice:** Nothing visible to customers — this is about making sure we know about problems before your customers do.

---

### May — Speed Improvements

Make the website load faster, especially product pages and the online menu. Add smooth loading animations so customers never see a blank screen while waiting.

**What you'll notice:** Pages feel snappier. Product photos and menus appear faster.

---

### June — Technology Upgrades

The tools that power your website have released newer versions with better performance and security. We'll carefully upgrade them one at a time to keep everything current.

**What you'll notice:** No visible changes for customers — this is like upgrading the engine in a car. Same car, better performance under the hood.

**Why it matters:** Outdated software eventually stops receiving security fixes and becomes harder to work with. Staying current now prevents a costly emergency upgrade later.

---

### July — Quality Assurance

Expand our automated testing to cover more of the website, especially the checkout and payment process. This means we catch problems before they reach your customers.

**What you'll notice:** Fewer bugs. More confidence when we make changes or add features. Faster turnaround on updates.

---

### August — Polish & Documentation

Clean up the codebase, improve internal documentation, and address smaller items that have been waiting. This makes future development faster and cheaper.

**What you'll notice:** No immediate visible changes — but future feature requests will be delivered faster and with fewer issues.

---

### September — Review & Plan Ahead

Run a full checkup on security, performance, and overall health. Compare results to where we started in April. Create a plan for the next improvement cycle.

**What you'll get:** A report similar to the one we delivered this month, showing progress and recommending next steps.

## What Success Looks Like

By September 2026, your website will have:

- **Faster page loads** — customers get to your content quicker
- **Stronger security** — protected against current and emerging threats
- **Up-to-date technology** — no outdated software creating risk

## Important Notes

- **No downtime expected.** All updates are deployed with zero interruption to your customers.
- **Priorities can shift.** If a business need comes up (new feature, seasonal promotion, etc.), we can adjust the schedule accordingly.
- **Urgent issues come first.** If a security issue or outage occurs, we address it immediately regardless of the plan.
- **Monthly check-ins recommended.** A quick 15-minute conversation each month to review progress and adjust priorities.
